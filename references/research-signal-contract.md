# Research Signal Contract

## Purpose

This contract defines how audience activation plans should handle modern research inputs such as synthetic panels, LLM interviews, surveys, experiments, and behavioral data.

The goal is to let the skill use AI-assisted research signals without overstating them. Synthetic consumers, LLM interviews, survey responses, and team assumptions can generate useful hypotheses. They do not create deterministic targetable audiences, and they do not prove causal treatment effects unless calibrated or validated with real behavior and an appropriate causal design.

## Core objects

### Research signal

A research signal is any input used to infer audience needs, preferences, channel fit, or likely response.

Allowed `source_type` values:

| Source type | Meaning | Default claim strength |
|---|---|---|
| `synthetic_panel` | Simulated or AI-generated respondents, personas, or synthetic consumers | Hypothesis-generating only |
| `llm_interview` | Interview-style output generated by or with an LLM | Hypothesis-generating only |
| `survey` | Stated responses from real respondents | Stated preference or descriptive segment difference |
| `experiment` | Randomized test, treatment/control design, or credible quasi-experiment | May support causal claims if design is sound |
| `sales_or_click_behavior` | Observed purchases, clicks, conversions, retention, or engagement | Observed behavioral association or lift |
| `expert_or_team_assumption` | Internal belief, market knowledge, sales input, or planning assumption | Hypothesis only |

### Evidence label

Allowed `evidence_label` values:

| Evidence label | Meaning | Allowed use |
|---|---|---|
| `descriptive_segment_lift` | A segment differs from another segment on a reported or observed metric | Build hypotheses, prioritize tests, design creative |
| `preference_heterogeneity` | Different audiences express or imply different preferences | Build message variants and landing-page variants |
| `behavioral_lift` | Observed behavioral difference in clicks, sales, conversion, retention, or engagement | Prioritize budget and proxy signals, subject to confounding caveats |
| `causal_hte` | Treatment effect heterogeneity backed by a real experiment, randomized test, or credible quasi-experiment | Use as stronger evidence for targeting and personalization |
| `uplift_targeting_label` | A deployable label or score from uplift modeling, validated on real behavior | Use as first-party or model-driven activation input, with monitoring |

## Allowed claims by source type

| Source type | Allowed evidence labels | Prohibited claims without extra evidence |
|---|---|---|
| `synthetic_panel` | `preference_heterogeneity`, `descriptive_segment_lift` when clearly framed as synthetic | Real consumer behavior, causal HTE, deterministic targeting inventory |
| `llm_interview` | `preference_heterogeneity`, `descriptive_segment_lift` when clearly framed as LLM-generated | Real consumer behavior, causal HTE, deterministic targeting inventory |
| `survey` | `preference_heterogeneity`, `descriptive_segment_lift` | Actual purchase behavior unless linked to behavior, causal HTE unless experimental |
| `sales_or_click_behavior` | `descriptive_segment_lift`, `behavioral_lift` | Causal HTE unless treatment/control or quasi-experiment exists |
| `experiment` | `descriptive_segment_lift`, `behavioral_lift`, `causal_hte` when design supports it | Broad generalization beyond tested population without caveats |
| `expert_or_team_assumption` | `preference_heterogeneity` only as hypothesis | Real behavior, causal HTE, validated audience label |

## Calibration levels

Use `calibration_level` to describe how close the signal is to real, deployable behavior.

| Calibration level | Meaning | Activation posture |
|---|---|---|
| `uncalibrated` | Not checked against real behavior | Small-budget validation only |
| `directionally_calibrated` | Direction checked against limited real data | Controlled tests with capped budget |
| `behaviorally_calibrated` | Validated against observed behavior | Can inform budget allocation, still monitor confounding |
| `experimentally_validated` | Validated through treatment/control, randomized test, or credible quasi-experiment | Stronger basis for scaling and personalization |

## Required fields for a research signal

```yaml
signal_id: <stable id>
source_type: synthetic_panel | llm_interview | survey | experiment | sales_or_click_behavior | expert_or_team_assumption
audience_description: <who the signal refers to>
finding: <what was learned>
evidence_label: descriptive_segment_lift | preference_heterogeneity | behavioral_lift | causal_hte | uplift_targeting_label
calibration_level: uncalibrated | directionally_calibrated | behaviorally_calibrated | experimentally_validated
limitations:
  - <known limitation>
recommended_use: <how this can safely be used in activation>
```

## Prohibited patterns

Do not write:

- Synthetic respondents prove real consumer behavior.
- LLM interviews show actual purchasing behavior.
- A platform can perfectly identify every person with a behavioral trait.
- A segment difference is causal HTE without treatment/control, randomized, or quasi-experimental evidence.
- A survey preference is the same as observed purchase lift.
- A custom audience signal guarantees delivery only to that exact audience unless the platform and data source explicitly support deterministic targeting.

## Safe language

Prefer:

- "This signal is hypothesis-generating."
- "Use this as a platform proxy and validate with a controlled test."
- "This row is based on observed behavior but does not prove causality."
- "This result supports a creative or audience-prioritization hypothesis."
- "This proxy should be capped until it clears the validation threshold."

## Example: synthetic panel input

```yaml
signal_id: syn_hu_cycling_001
source_type: synthetic_panel
audience_description: Hungarian urban cycling-interested consumers
finding: Synthetic respondents who described themselves as urban commuters preferred messages about time saving, theft prevention, and night visibility.
evidence_label: preference_heterogeneity
calibration_level: uncalibrated
limitations:
  - Synthetic respondents are not real observed buyers.
  - The signal may reflect prompt design and model priors.
recommended_use: Generate creative hypotheses for commuter cycling ads and validate with small-budget Search, Meta, TikTok, and landing-page tests.
```

Safe activation interpretation:

```text
Urban commuter cyclists may respond to convenience and safety messaging. Use city cycling keywords, bike lock and light product pages, cycling creator content, and Budapest-focused creative as proxy signals. Validate through conversion, add-to-cart, or qualified-visit lift before scaling.
```

## Example: real experiment input

```yaml
signal_id: exp_hu_cycling_002
source_type: experiment
audience_description: Hungarian website visitors who viewed cycling accessory pages
treatment: Safety bundle landing page
control: Generic accessories landing page
finding: Treatment increased purchase conversion among visitors who had viewed helmet or light pages before the test.
evidence_label: causal_hte
calibration_level: experimentally_validated
limitations:
  - Applies to website visitors in the tested period.
  - May not generalize to cold audiences or other countries.
recommended_use: Prioritize safety-bundle retargeting for helmet and light page viewers; test incremental lift before expanding to cold prospecting.
```

Safe activation interpretation:

```text
For users with first-party helmet or light browsing behavior, safety-bundle messaging has causal evidence within the tested website population. Use remarketing and CRM segmentation first, then run a separate prospecting test before broad scaling.
```
